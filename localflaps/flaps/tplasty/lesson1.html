T-PLASTY
LESSON 1/8
Description
  • Bilateral unipedicular flap using single incisions on each side of a defect to advance the skin to achieve closure
  • May be called O-T or A-T depending on the shape of the original defect
  • The conventional O-T flap design employs the tangent of a circular defect as the incision, with the tension center localized at the closure site of opposing sides of the circular defect
  • The modified O-T flap uses the evenly distributes the circular defect, utilizes the long axis as the transverse incision for a T-shaped flap, and designates the defect center as the tension center. This approach effectively disperses tension, alleviating the stress on each advancement flap.
